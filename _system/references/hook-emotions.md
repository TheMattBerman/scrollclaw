# Hook Emotions

The first 2-3 seconds is a face with an emotion. This is what stops the scroll — not the text, not the product. The face.

## Emotion categories (ranked by engagement)

| Emotion | Face expression | Best for | Hook text pairing |
|---------|----------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Shocked** | Wide eyes, open mouth, head slightly back | Surprising stats, reveals, "wait what" moments | "[Stat] people don't know this..." |
| **Crying/Tears** | Watery eyes, slight frown, vulnerability | Emotional pain points, relatable struggles | "When [painful relatable situation]..." |
| **Frustrated** | Furrowed brow, tight jaw, exhale | Problem-agitate-solve, "I'm done with this" | "I'm so tired of [specific frustration]" |
| **Angry** | Intense eyes, set jaw, slight head forward | Injustice, call-to-action, righteous anger | "Nobody talks about [hidden truth]" |
| **Happy/Excited** | Genuine smile, bright eyes, slight lean forward | Wins, discoveries, positive outcomes | "I finally found [solution]" |
| **Confused** | Squinted eyes, tilted head, slight frown | Educational, myth-busting, "this makes no sense" | "Why does [counterintuitive thing] happen?" |

## How to use with Sora

The hook face is a 2-5 second clip generated with Sora i2v:

1. **Generate the face with Nano Banana** — creator in the right setting, wearing the right expression. The expression is the ENTIRE prompt focus. Everything else (background, lighting) should be simple and not compete with the face.

2. **Animate minimally with Sora** — the face doesn't need to talk. Subtle movement: a blink, a slight head shake, eyes widening slightly. The text overlay carries the hook message, not the voice.

3. **Burn hook text on top** — using green zone positioning and platform-native fonts. The text + face create the scroll-stop together.

4. **Cut to demo/B-roll** — after 3-5 seconds, hard cut to the product demo, talking head, or whatever format follows.

## First-frame prompts for each emotion

### Shocked
```
[creator identity block]
Expression: wide eyes, eyebrows raised, mouth slightly open in genuine surprise. 
Not exaggerated — real shock, like they just read something unexpected on their phone.
The surprise is in the eyes, not the mouth.
```

### Frustrated  
```
[creator identity block]
Expression: slight furrowed brow, jaw set, mildly exhausted.
Not angry — frustrated. The face of someone who has tried everything and is running out of patience.
One hand may be rubbing temple or resting on chin.
```

### Angry
```
[creator identity block]
Expression: intense direct eye contact, set jaw, head slightly forward.
Controlled anger — not screaming, not red-faced. The "I've had enough" look.
Lips pressed together, nostrils slightly flared.
```

### Happy/Excited
```
[creator identity block]
Expression: genuine smile starting to form, bright eyes, slight lean forward.
Not a posed smile — the face of someone who just discovered something that actually works.
Slight disbelief mixed with relief.
```

### Confused
```
[creator identity block]
Expression: eyes slightly squinted, head tilted to one side, slight frown.
Processing something that doesn't make sense. Looking at phone or laptop screen.
"Wait, what?" energy.
```

### Crying/Tears
```
[creator identity block]
Expression: eyes glistening, lower lip slightly trembling, vulnerable.
Not sobbing — the quiet emotional moment. Relatable pain, not drama.
May be looking slightly down or to the side.
```

## The hook + demo structure

```
[HOOK: 2-5s — emotive face + text overlay]
   ↓ hard cut
[DEMO: 10-13s — product in action, talking head, or B-roll]
   Total: 15s max
```

This is the proven format from operators running high-volume UGC. The hook face catches attention at the visual processing level (pre-conscious). The text gives the conscious brain a reason to stay. The demo delivers the content.

## Emotion selection guide

| If the product solves... | Use this emotion |
|-------------------------|-----------------|
| A painful ongoing problem | Frustrated or Crying |
| Something people don't know about | Shocked or Confused |
| A problem people are angry about | Angry |
| Something that genuinely works | Happy/Excited (but only AFTER establishing the problem) |

**Rule:** lead with negative emotions (frustrated, angry, shocked) for cold traffic. Save happy/excited for the payoff or warm audiences. Negative emotions stop the scroll harder than positive ones.
